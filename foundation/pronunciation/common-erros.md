# Common Pronunciation Errors (English)

## 1. Vowel Confusion

### Problem
Mixing similar vowel sounds.

### Examples
- ship /ʃɪp/ vs sheep /ʃiːp/
- full /fʊl/ vs fool /fuːl/
- bad /bæd/ vs bed /bed/

### Tips
- Short vowels are **short and relaxed**
- Long vowels are **tense and longer**
- Practice minimal pairs daily

---

## 2. Missing or Incorrect Stress

### Problem
Incorrect word stress makes speech hard to understand.

### Examples
- PREsent (noun) vs preSENT (verb)
- IMportant (correct) ❌ imPORtant

### Tips
- Every word has ONE main stress
- Stress = louder + longer + clearer
- Check dictionary phonetics

---

## 3. Dropping Final Consonants

### Problem
Not pronouncing ending sounds clearly.

### Examples
- bad → ba
- stop → sto
- hand → han

### Tips
- English is **consonant-heavy**
- Always finish the word
- Practice exaggerating endings

---

## 4. Confusing /l/ and /r/

### Problem
Mixing these two sounds.

### Examples
- light vs right
- glass vs grass

### Tips
- /l/: tongue touches upper teeth
- /r/: tongue does NOT touch
- Practice slowly with mirror

---

## 5. Confusing /θ/ and /ð/ (th sounds)

### Problem
Replacing with /s/, /z/, or /d/

### Examples
- think → sink ❌
- this → dis ❌

### Tips
- Put tongue BETWEEN teeth
- Blow air gently
- Practice: think, thank, this, that

---

## 6. Ignoring Weak Forms (Schwa /ə/)

### Problem
Pronouncing every word strongly

### Examples
- to → /tuː/ ❌ → /tə/ ✅
- for → /fɔː/ ❌ → /fə/ ✅

### Tips
- Most unstressed syllables = /ə/
- English rhythm depends on weak sounds

---

## 7. No Linking Between Words

### Problem
Speaking word-by-word

### Examples
- pick it up → pick-it-up
- go on → go-won

### Tips
- English is **connected speech**
- Link consonant → vowel

---

## 8. Flat Intonation

### Problem
No rise/fall in tone

### Examples
- Statement: falling tone ↓
- Question: rising tone ↑

### Tips
- English is NOT flat like Chinese
- Practice sentence melody

---

## 9. Overpronouncing Silent Letters

### Problem
Pronouncing letters that should be silent

### Examples
- knife → /naɪf/ (k silent)
- debt → /det/ (b silent)

### Tips
- Memorize common silent letters
- Trust phonetic symbols, not spelling

---

## 10. Incorrect Consonant Clusters

### Problem
Adding vowels inside clusters

### Examples
- stop → /sətop/ ❌
- school → /səkul/ ❌

### Tips
- Do NOT insert extra vowels
- Practice slow blending: s + t + op

---

## 11. Overusing Chinese Pronunciation Patterns

### Problem
Applying Chinese sound rules to English

### Examples
- No consonant endings
- No stress differences

### Tips
- English = stress-timed language
- Chinese = syllable-timed
- Train rhythm separately

---

## 12. Not Using Mouth Properly

### Problem
Speaking with minimal mouth movement

### Tips
- Open mouth more
- Exaggerate at practice stage
- English needs clear articulation

---

## Daily Practice Routine

1. Minimal pairs (10 min)
2. Shadowing native audio (10 min)
3. Read aloud with stress (10 min)
4. Record and compare (5 min)

---

## Key Principle

> Pronunciation is not about individual sounds only —  
it is about **stress + rhythm + connection + clarity**.
