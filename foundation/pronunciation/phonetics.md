# English Phonetics Summary

## 1. What is Phonetics?

Phonetics is the study of **speech sounds** in a language.

👉 It focuses on:

* how sounds are produced
* how they are pronounced
* how they are heard

---

## 2. Why Phonetics Matters

* Improve pronunciation
* Reduce accent issues
* Speak more clearly and confidently
* Understand native speakers better

---

## 3. The IPA (International Phonetic Alphabet)

👉 IPA is a system used to represent sounds.

Example:

* cat → /kæt/
* dog → /dɔːɡ/

👉 Each symbol represents a specific sound

---

## 4. Types of Sounds

### 🔹 Vowels

👉 Produced without blocking airflow

#### Short vowels:

* /ɪ/ (sit)
* /e/ (bed)
* /æ/ (cat)
* /ʌ/ (cup)
* /ɒ/ (hot)

#### Long vowels:

* /iː/ (see)
* /ɑː/ (car)
* /ɔː/ (law)
* /uː/ (blue)
* /ɜː/ (bird)

---

### 🔹 Consonants

👉 Produced with airflow obstruction

Examples:

* /p/ (pen)
* /b/ (boy)
* /t/ (top)
* /d/ (dog)
* /k/ (cat)
* /g/ (go)

---

## 5. Stress (Very Important)

👉 Stress = emphasis on a syllable

Example:

* PREsent (noun)
* preSENT (verb)

👉 Wrong stress can change meaning

---

## 6. Intonation

👉 Intonation = rise and fall of voice

* Rising tone → questions
* Falling tone → statements

Example:

* Are you coming? ⬆
* I am coming. ⬇

---

## 7. Connected Speech

👉 Native speakers link words together

Examples:

* want to → wanna
* going to → gonna

---

## 8. Common Pronunciation Problems

* Not stressing correct syllables
* Mispronouncing vowel sounds
* Speaking too flat (no intonation)
* Not linking words

---

## 9. Practical Tips

* Learn IPA for common words
* Practice speaking out loud
* Listen and imitate native speakers
* Record and correct yourself

---

## 10. One-Sentence Summary

> Phonetics is the study of speech sounds, helping learners improve pronunciation, stress, and natural speaking patterns.
