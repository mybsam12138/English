# English Collocations Summary

## 1. What are Collocations?

Collocations are **natural combinations of words** that are commonly used together by native speakers.

👉 Instead of choosing words randomly, English uses **fixed or preferred combinations**.

---

## 2. Why Collocations Matter

* Make your English sound **natural**
* Improve **fluency**
* Avoid unnatural expressions
* Help in both **speaking and writing**

---

## 3. Common Types of Collocations

### 🔹 Verb + Noun

* make a decision
* do homework
* take a break
* give advice

---

### 🔹 Adjective + Noun

* heavy rain
* strong coffee
* big problem
* high risk

---

### 🔹 Verb + Adverb

* run quickly
* speak clearly
* work efficiently

---

### 🔹 Adverb + Adjective

* very important
* highly effective
* deeply concerned

---

### 🔹 Noun + Noun

* data analysis
* system design
* project management

---

## 4. Correct vs Incorrect Examples

### ✅ Correct:

* make a decision
* heavy traffic
* strong argument

---

### ❌ Incorrect:

* do a decision
* strong traffic
* powerful argument

👉 Even if grammar is correct, it may sound unnatural

---

## 5. Collocations in Work / Tech Context (Important for You 👍)

* solve a problem
* build a system
* design a solution
* improve performance
* handle requests
* process data

---

## 6. Key Rule

👉 Collocations must be **memorized and practiced**

There is often **no strict grammar rule**

---

## 7. Learning Tips

* Learn words in **phrases, not individually**
* Pay attention to **real usage**
* Practice speaking using common patterns

---

## 8. Speaking Upgrade

### ❌ Basic:

* do a solution

### ✅ Better:

* provide a solution
* design a solution

---

## 9. Common Patterns to Remember

* make + decision / plan / mistake
* take + action / responsibility / time
* give + advice / support
* have + problem / idea

---

## 10. One-Sentence Summary

> Collocations are natural word combinations that make English sound fluent and correct, and they should be learned as phrases rather than individual words.
