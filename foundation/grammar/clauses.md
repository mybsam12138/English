# English Grammar: Clauses Summary

## 1. What is a Clause?

A **clause** is a group of words that contains:

* a **subject**
* a **verb**

👉 It can express a complete idea or part of a sentence.

---

## 2. Types of Clauses

There are two main types:

### 🔹 1. Independent Clause

> A clause that can stand alone as a complete sentence.

Example:

* I finished my work.
* She is studying English.

✅ Has full meaning
✅ Can be a sentence by itself

---

### 🔹 2. Dependent Clause (Subordinate Clause)

> A clause that cannot stand alone and depends on another clause.

Example:

* because I was tired
* if it rains
* when he arrives

❌ Not a complete sentence
✅ Needs an independent clause

---

## 3. Common Types of Dependent Clauses

### 🔸 3.1 Noun Clause

Acts like a **noun** in a sentence.

Example:

* I know **that he is coming**.
* What she said is important.

👉 Can be subject, object, or complement

---

### 🔸 3.2 Adjective Clause (Relative Clause)

Describes a **noun**.

Usually starts with:

* who, which, that, where

Example:

* The book **that you gave me** is interesting.
* The person **who called you** is my friend.

---

### 🔸 3.3 Adverb Clause

Describes a **verb**, adjective, or sentence.

Usually starts with:

* because, if, when, although, while, since

Example:

* I stayed home **because I was sick**.
* If it rains, we will cancel the trip.

---

## 4. Clause vs Phrase

| Feature            | Clause         | Phrase       |
| ------------------ | -------------- | ------------ |
| Has subject + verb | ✅ Yes          | ❌ No         |
| Can express idea   | Yes            | Partial      |
| Example            | she is running | running fast |

---

## 5. Simple vs Complex Sentences

### 🔹 Simple Sentence

* One independent clause

Example:

* I work every day.

---

### 🔹 Compound Sentence

* Two independent clauses

Example:

* I work hard, and I earn money.

---

### 🔹 Complex Sentence

* One independent + one dependent clause

Example:

* I work hard because I need money.

---

## 6. Key Signal Words

### Noun Clause:

* that, what, whether

### Adjective Clause:

* who, which, that

### Adverb Clause:

* because, if, when, although, while

---

## 7. Practical Tips

* Use clauses to make sentences more natural and fluent
* Avoid too many short simple sentences
* Combine ideas using dependent clauses

---

## 8. Interview / Speaking Tip

👉 Use clauses to sound more natural:

Instead of:

* “I was tired. I went home.”

Say:

* “I went home because I was tired.”

---

## 9. One-Sentence Summary

> A clause is a group of words with a subject and verb, and it can be either independent (complete) or dependent (incomplete).
